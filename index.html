The status dropdown is available at the top of every chat window. Click on the current status to update it to Open, Waiting On Agent, or Closed.

On Robylon, every chat has one of three statuses. By default, all newly assigned chats will have an Open status. You must update the status based on the situation as the conversation progresses.

The Three Chat Statuses
Open Default status for all active chats. A chat is Open when you are actively handling it and the conversation is in progress. Keep it Open until there is a specific reason to change it.
Waiting on Agent Use this whenever something is pending, whether it is on our side (researching, checking with internal teams, buying time) OR on the customer's side (waiting for them to share a screenshot, document, or reply). This is the single "on hold" status for both scenarios.
Closed Use when: (a) the issue is fully resolved and the customer has confirmed it, (b) closing after no response following your formal follow-up, or (c) closing a junk chat. All chats must end as Closed.
Quick Guide — When to Set Each Status
Set Waiting on Agent when… You are verifying details or waiting for a response from our team, OR you have asked the customer to share something (screenshot, transaction ID, bank statement) and are awaiting their response. Also set this right before logging off for the day.
Set Closed when… The issue is solved and confirmed by the customer, OR there is no response after a follow-up (apply the 'No Response' tag), OR it is a junk chat (apply the 'Junk' tag). All closures are set to Closed.
Important
Do not leave chats on Open unnecessarily when an item is pending. Update to Waiting on Agent as soon as there is a hold, whether it is on your side or the customer's side.

Chat Opening Protocols

As soon as a chat is assigned, verify whether the customer has already shared a specific query or just a generic greeting.

⏳ Scenario A

No query yet, just "Hi", "Hello", or blank text

  • 1Send a warm, personalized greeting within 20–30 minutes.
  • 2Wait for the customer to share their query details.
  • 3If no reply comes in, follow the Silence Handling steps.
Sample Greeting

Hi! I am Manorathi from Wint Wealth. Hope you are doing well. How can I help you today?

Scenario B

Customer has already shared a specific query

  • 1Send your formal greeting first as a separate, clean message.
  • 2Then, follow up with an acknowledgement or answer in a second message.
  • 3Do not ignore the query. Proceed immediately to Step 3 (Active Handling).
Sample Greeting then Acknowledgement

Hi! I am Manorathi from Wint Wealth. Hope you are doing well.

I have received your query regarding [topic]. Kindly allow me a few moments to check the details for you.

Important
Always send your greeting as a separate message first. Then send the acknowledgement or answer as a second message. Do not combine both into one massive block of text, even if you already know the answer.

Handling Customer Silence (No Response)

This applies when the customer does not reply after your first greeting, as well as when they go silent mid-chat. Follow the exact same sequence in both cases.

Wait 20–30 mins after your last message Do not rush the user. Give the customer enough time to read your text and respond.
Send a personalized follow-up message Always reference the customer's specific topic. Do not send a completely generic template.
Sample Follow-Up (initial silence)

Sir/Madam, just checking if we are still connected? Kindly let me know your query whenever you are ready, I am here to assist you.

Sample Follow-Up (mid-chat silence)

I hope the details shared above helped clarify your query regarding [topic]. Please let me know if you have any further questions; I will be happy to help.

Process Check
If the platform has already sent an automated follow-up ping (after 40–45 minutes), do NOT send an additional manual follow-up message. That would be a process deviation.
Wait another 30 mins after your follow-up Allow sufficient time for a response before moving to closure.
Still no response? Close the interaction. Update the chat status to Closed and add the 'Junk No Response' tag.

Active Query Handling Sequence

Once the user shares their question, resolve it methodically by following this exact standard sequence.

  • 1
    Read the entire query with full attention before replying.
    Understand exactly what the investor is looking for. Do not jump to a quick reply or assume details based on just the first few words.
  • 2
    Do you know the exact technical answer?
    If yes: Send your greeting first, then share the complete answer in a separate message. Make sure it is accurate and properly formatted.
    If no: Send a personalized acknowledgement right away and change the chat status to Waiting on Agent.
    Acknowledgement — Verifying Details

    Sir, I have gone through your query regarding the referral benefits. Kindly allow me a brief moment to verify your account details and update you.

    Acknowledgement — Checking with Team

    I have received your query about your bank account linking. Kindly allow me a few minutes to check this with our backend operations team and get back to you.

    Set status: Waiting on Agent
    Process Breach
    Failing to set the chat status to Waiting on Agent after sending an acknowledgement is considered a severe process breach.
  • 3
    Do you need additional info/inputs from the customer?
    If your evaluation depends on information from the user (such as a payment proof, screen capture, or statement), request it politely and set the chat to Waiting on Agent.
    Sample — Requesting Information from Customer

    Sir, to help me investigate this further, could you kindly share a screenshot of the error page you are seeing? This will help me resolve this for you at the earliest.

    Set status: Waiting on Agent
    Note
    The Waiting on Agent status is universally used both when WE are researching details and when we are WAITING for the customer to share inputs. It serves as our single pending holder.
  • 4
    Draft your final response.
    Use the internal Writer tool to format and refine your message. Address all parts of the user's queries, mirror their vocabulary, break down numbers into points, attach accurate links/screenshots, and verify information. If you are ever unsure, consult your SME or TL. Never guess.
  • 5
    Review the entire chat text before hitting send.
    Verify that no ancillary question has been left unaddressed. Ensure your tone is helpful, complete, and respectful.
  • 6
    Deliver the final solution within 1 hour of acknowledgement.
    Copy your polished text from the tool and send it out. Do not mix your final resolution message with follow-up phrasing.
  • 7
    Customer has cross-questions?
    Loop back to the top of this active sequence. Continue addressing inputs until all issues are fully resolved.

Junk Chat Filtering Rules

A chat thread is officially categorized as a 'Junk Chat' in the following scenarios. Treat them politely, apply closure text, and close as Closed with the Junk tag.

  • The incoming payload contains only unknown external links, spam screenshots, or random text blasts.
  • The investor drops in only to mention that their issue was completely resolved before your chat opened.
  • The context is random or text is completely unrelated to Wint Wealth services or accounts.
Send a formal template closure note and update status to Closed with Junk tag

Platform Core Rule — All Chats End as Closed
Regardless of the reason for ending the conversation (whether it is successfully solved, left unanswered, or filtered as spam), the final chat status in the system must always be updated to Closed.

Resolution Status — Resolved Toggle (Right Sidebar)

Mandatory Field Selection

Along with moving the primary Chat Status header to Closed, you must update the Resolved parameter property inside the Properties panel on the right-side toolbar sidebar before saving and exiting. This is a separate metric item used for tracking operational quality.

Robylon UI — Properties Panel (Right Sidebar)
Ticket Properties
Yes
No

The Resolved dropdown toggle is in the Properties panel on the right sidebar. Select Yes or No based on whether the query was actually resolved, then click Save Properties.

Set Resolved → YES when…
Query is fully resolved Customer confirmed the answer, said "Thank you", "Okay", or "Got it". The issue is genuinely sorted and the customer is satisfied.
Call resolved the query A call was connected, the query was answered on the call, and the post-call summary has been sent on chat.
Set Resolved → NO when…
No response from customer Customer did not reply after your follow-up. Chat is being closed due to silence — the query was not confirmed as resolved by the customer.
Junk chat Irrelevant message, unknown link, or already resolved before chat started. No actual query was handled in this conversation.
Call not answered You attempted the call but it went unanswered. The customer did not connect and no resolution was provided.
Two Separate Steps to Close a Chat on Robylon
Closing a chat correctly requires two separate actions:
Step 1: Set the Chat Status to Closed using the dropdown at the top of the chat window.
Step 2: Set Resolved → Yes or No in the Properties panel on the right sidebar, then click Save.
Do not skip either step.

Shift Ending Protocol

If your shift is about to end and there are open chats, follow these steps in exact order. Never log off without completing all steps.

1
Acknowledge the last customer message in each open chat.
2
Send a personalised logging-off message to the customer.
3
Set the chat status to Waiting on Agent.
4
Change your Agent Availability to Unavailable (Offline).
Sample Logging-Off Message

I am logging off for the day, but your chat is still open with us. I will update you as soon as I receive a response from the concerned team. Feel free to leave any further questions right here; our team will check them and reply as soon as we back online tomorrow morning at 10 AM. Thank you for your understanding, and have a good evening!

Order Matters
Change availability to Unavailable only after all log-off messages are sent and all open chats are set to Waiting on Agent. Do not go Unavailable before completing these steps.

Communication Standards

These standards apply to every single message you send. There are no exceptions.

Always Do This

  • Use simple, clear language — avoid jargon
  • Use proper grammar and punctuation in every message
  • Break long answers into smaller, easy-to-read points
  • Use words like "Apologies" and "Thank you" appropriately
  • Wish the customer on festivals and weekends when relevant
  • Appreciate the user when they share requested details
  • Include the user's query wording in your response
  • Consult SME or TL when unsure of the answer

Never Do This

  • Use words like suggestion, recommendation, advice
  • Ask for bank statements or CMR documents over chat
  • Provide incomplete or partial answers
  • Ignore any query — even a minor one
  • Send copy-paste robotic responses
  • Send one-word replies
  • Guess an answer you are not sure about
  • Rush to close a chat before all queries are resolved

Personalisation Example

"I hope I was able to address all your queries with the solutions provided above."

Educational Responsibility
Your role is to educate users, not advise them. Clarify doubts, explain processes, and guide them through platform usage. Give the right amount of information do not overwhelm.
